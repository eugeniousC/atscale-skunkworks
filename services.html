How engagements actually run

  • One scheduled 60–90 minute working session per week. Same day, same time, recurring. Phone-silent, agenda-driven, no placeholder meetings.
  • Async deep work between sessions. Slack or email for routine communication. The weekly session is the heartbeat.
  • Hybrid analyst-architect posture. I drive the analysis. Your team brings the context. We co-produce the deliverables. Ownership of the work transfers to your team at engagement close — you must be able to run the framework forward without me. I never become the single point of failure for your revenue system.
  • Every engagement closes with three artifacts you own. Diagnostic Readout · Recommendation Architecture · Adoption Playbook. Standardized spine, bespoke content. You leave with documents your team uses, not slides that gather dust.

This is fractional-exec posture, not done-for-you consulting. I’m not your operator. I’m the architect you bring in when your operator needs a system to run.

Coexists with your other advisors. Most of my clients also work with a Vistage Chair, an EOS Implementer, a fractional CFO, or a business coach. Different muscle, different role. I’m not here to replace those professionals — I’m here to fix the structural leak underneath the system they’re running. The work compounds when the foundation is honest.

What I don’t do

  • No hourly billing. Ever. Value-anchored fixed prices on every engagement.
  • No retainer-by-default. Lead with fixed-scope projects (4–12 weeks). Retainers are earned by client pull, not pushed.
  • No mid-engagement scope drift. If the scope changes, we close the current engagement and rescope the sequel.
  • No engagements without a named outcome. Every contract names the specific friction we’re solving and how we’ll both know we’re done.
  • No “let me think about it for 30+ days” pressure. If now isn’t the right time, I’ll re-engage in 60 days. Push from me before that signals desperation, not professionalism.
