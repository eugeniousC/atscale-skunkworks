Why I do this

Somewhere around year 18, I noticed something.

The executives I was helping at $500M and $2B companies were solving the same five problems I see now in $5M businesses. Broken handoffs. Forecast theater. Revenue leaking through operational seams. Owners who couldn’t predict next quarter. Teams who couldn’t move without the CEO in the room.

The only differences at enterprise scale were better software and a louder PR machine. The underlying problems were the same. The fixes were the same.

Most SMB owners can’t afford a McKinsey engagement, and they shouldn’t want one — McKinsey would charge them $400K to read them their own playbook back. What they need is somebody who’s seen the failure mode at every scale, can name it in dollars, and can fix the foundation before AI accelerates the breakage.

That’s what atScale Advisors is.

I’m an architect, not a coach. Coaches build people. Implementers install operating systems. Peer groups give you counsel. Those are real jobs done by real professionals — I’m not one of them. I’m the person you bring in when the system is what’s breaking, not the team running it. The diagnosis is structural. The fix is architectural. The output is a plan your team can run without me in the room.
